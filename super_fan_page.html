Franchises and Mascots

Companies are great and all, but you can't become a household-name company unless you have something that defines you. This is where their games come in, letting their expertise in gameplay and design really push the limits. I'll be talking more about the virtual side of things, seeing as they are more involved in mainstream media nowadays, and because I'm also biased.

It is impossible to talk about any sort of game without thinking about nintendo's most iconic character, Mario Jumpman Mario. he's an iconic character that almost anyone can recognize instantly, and was the one to save the dying video-game market. Without mario, perhaps technology might not be what it is today. He started out as a simple platformer, but overtime branched off into different types of games like kart racing and party games.

Minecraft. One of the popular and most profitable games of all time, gaining mojang and microsoft billions of dollars over the past decade. It introduced the idea of extreme online multiplayer, able to hold hundreds or even thousands of players in any given server at a time. It started off small back in 2009-2010, being a simple block generation game with minor building capabilities, but overtime became more. More blocks were added to the inventory, and the game was overall refined over and over to better suit the evergrowing fanbase.

How Games are created

I obviously don't know the finer details of game creation, but I believe I know enough to guide any casual viewer with interest through the steps.

  1. Idea
    • Think of a general gameplay idea or story you want to express in your game.
  2. Characters
    • Create interesting characters, and give them rolls like NPCs, protagonist(s), or antagonist(s).
  3. Story
    • Think of a story that would want to entice the players and make them want to leaarn more about to keep going.
  4. Mechanics
    • Think of fun, but never overused gimmicks that you could use to keeep your gameplay fresh, fun, and functional.
  5. Graphics
    • Design a wide veriety of textures, colors, and designs inside your game.
  6. Marketing
    • Make sure the word about your game is out there, and is catching on. Only get to this step when development is almost to completion.
  7. Publshing
    • Make sure your game is up to code, before sending it off into the world for anyone and everyone to enjoy.
